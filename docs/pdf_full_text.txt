Self-exciting point processes have been used in crime modeling with

Clustering Processes
1. Log-Gaussian Cox Process (LGCP):
A doubly stochastic process where the intensity function О»(s) follows
a log-Gaussian random field.
Captures overdispersion and spatial heterogeneity.
Points cluster around high-intensity areas.
2. Thomas Process:
Cluster process with parent points generated by a Poisson process.
Offspring points are normally distributed around each parent.
Results in circular clusters with random centers.
3. MatВҙern Cluster Process:
Similar to Thomas, but offspring are uniformly distributed in a disc
around each parent.
Simpler spatial structure with fixed cluster radius.

Implications for Statistical Inference
Consequences of intractable likelihoods:
Maximum likelihood estimation becomes unavailable.
Bayesian inference (posterior computation) is intractable.
Model comparison via AIC/BIC or marginal likelihood is compromised.
Practically: We must rely on alternative inference strategies:
Pseudo-likelihood and composite likelihood.
Simulation-based methods (Monte Carlo, MCMC, ABC).
Classification-based surrogates or contrastive approaches.
